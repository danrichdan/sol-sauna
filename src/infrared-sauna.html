What is an Infrared Sauna?

An infrared sauna uses infrared light waves, to heat the body from the inside out. The IR waves actually penetrate the epidermis and dermis layers and gets into the body’s deep cells and tissues. The IR saunas create an artificial fever as it raises the body’s core temperature by 2-4 degrees. This fever, emulates our own body’s immune mechanism as fevers are created to fight infection, remove toxins, and kill off fhe bad cells.

Our Saunas

The saunas at Sol Sauna are full spectrum saunas. They use Near Infrared, Mid Infrared and Far Infrared waves together to get the maximum benefits of each. Each session is up to 40 minutes long in a private room with a private sauna, where each user is exposed under mild conditions to the benefits of infrared.

Types of Infrared

Near Infrared

Near Infrared is the shortest wavelength, and will be absorbed just below the surface of the skin creating a sweat that promotes healing and revitalization.

Mid Infrared

Mid infrared is a longer wavelength that can penetrate deeper into the body’s soft tissue increasing circulation, releasing oxygen to reach injured areas.

Far Infrared

Far infrared is the longest wavelength which penetrates the fat cells causing vasodilation, where the fat cells vibrate to expel toxins, resulting in the greatest levels of detoxification and stimulate your metabolism to aid in weight-loss.

Infrared versus Traditional saunas

Infrared Saunas use radiant heat, to heat from the core out. Whereas, traditional saunas use convectional heat. It heats the air in the sauna unit, which then heats the body. This type of sauna does not actually penetrate the dermis layers. It is the deep reach of the infrared waves that makes infrared saunas up to 7 times more effective than traditional saunas in terms of shedding toxins and fat cells.

Infrared saunas are able to heat the core and achieve its benefits at much lower temps. The optimal operating temperature is 110F-130F (although many chose to operate them at higher temps closer to 150F, just to feel more of the exterior heat). Many users are able to endure a complete 40 min session without any major discomfort or the need to run out of the sauna session.

Traditional saunas operate at temperatures above 185F, making it difficult for users to stay in the environment for any length of time.

Note** Infrared Waves are not to be mistaken as UltraViolet waves. UV waves are the ones that do damage to the skin, and are related to melanoma type cancers.
